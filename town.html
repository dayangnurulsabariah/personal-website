The land below the wind: Sabah, The Land Below the Wind, is located on the island of Borneo and is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural diversity, and the iconic Mount Kinabalu. It is called “The Land Below the Wind” because it lies just south of the typhoon belt, making it a peaceful and serene destination, often spared from violent storms. Sabah offers visitors a unique experience with its lush rainforests, beautiful islands, and warm, hospitable people.

Etnik Sabah (Bajau Samah): Kota Kinabalu, the capital city of Sabah, is a vibrant coastal city facing the South China Sea. It serves as the main gateway to Sabah and is known for its blend of modern development and natural charm. This is the beautiful town I love—my hometown is Inanam, one of the districts here. I’ve lived here from birth until the age of 20, and I’ve never moved—just stayed in the same home all this time.

Kota kinabalu: I’m from the Bajau Samah ethnic group, one of the many indigenous communities in Sabah. Bajau Samah people are known for their rich traditions, respect for elders, and beautiful cultural attire. We often speak in our own dialect, and family ties are very important to us. Many Bajau Samah live along the west coast of Sabah, especially around Kota Kinabalu and nearby areas like Inanam, also KB and Tuaran.
